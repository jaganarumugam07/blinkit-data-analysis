# Blinkit Data Analysis – Insights Report

This report outlines the major business insights generated from the Blinkit dataset through SQL analysis and Power BI visualizations. Source queries and requirements are based on the uploaded Business Requirement and Query Documentation files.

---

##  1. Overall Performance KPIs

| KPI | Insight |
|-----|---------|
| **Total Sales** | Strong revenue distribution across all outlets, with significant contribution from Regular-fat items. |
| **Average Sales** | Average revenue per item suggests consistent customer spend across categories. |
| **Number of Items Sold** | Large volume of unique items indicates wide product variety and high customer demand. |
| **Average Rating** | Customer satisfaction remains stable with moderate-to-high overall ratings. |

---

##  2. Performance by Fat Content

- **Regular Fat products generate higher total sales** than Low Fat.
- Low Fat products show **higher customer rating variations**, indicating selective preference.
- Product mix optimization could focus on expanding Regular Fat SKUs.

---

##  3. Total Sales by Item Type

Top-performing categories include:

1. **Snack Foods**
2. **Dairy Products**
3. **Household Items**
4. **Frozen Foods**

These categories drive majority of sales and should remain primary inventory focus.

Low-performing categories (e.g., Breakfast, Seafood) could benefit from improved promotions or inventory realignment.

---

##  4. Fat Content by Outlet Performance

- Outlets in Tier 2 and Tier 3 locations show **higher sales for both fat categories**.
- Sales variance indicates:
  - Tier 3 outlets: Larger customer base, better footfall.
  - Tier 1 outlets: Lower demand for certain grocery staples.

---

##  5. Sales by Outlet Establishment Year

- Older outlets (1998–2000) show **higher cumulative sales**.
- Newer outlets (2007–2009) show **fast-growing revenue**, suggesting ongoing expansion.

This indicates Blinkit’s ability to scale in newer markets effectively.

---

##  6. Sales Contribution by Outlet Size

- **Medium-sized outlets contribute the highest percentage (40%–50%)** of total sales.
- Small outlets have lower sales share but **higher efficiency per sq. ft**.
- Large outlets underperform relative to size, suggesting optimization opportunities.

---

##  7. Sales by Outlet Location Type

- **Tier 3 locations lead in total sales**, likely due to:
  - Fewer competitors
  - Local grocery demand
  - Better outlet penetration

Tier 1 locations show smaller revenue but better product diversity.

---

##  8. All Metrics by Outlet Type

Supermarkets lead in:

- Total Sales  
- Average Sales per item  
- Number of items sold  

Grocery stores lag behind, suggesting potential improvement via:

- Better assortment
- Promotional campaigns
- Improved customer experience

---

##  Key Business Recommendations

### 1. **Strengthen inventory for top-selling categories**
Expand stock for Snacks, Dairy, and Household items in high-performing outlets.

### 2. **Increase visibility for low-performing categories**
Introduce:
- Discounts  
- Bundles  
- Push notifications via app  

### 3. **Boost supply and marketing in Tier 3 outlets**
Since they already drive high sales volume.

### 4. **Optimize large outlets**
Reduce low-velocity SKUs or repurpose space.

### 5. ** Expand Regular Fat product line**
Based on consistently higher sales contribution.

---

##  Conclusion

This analysis highlights critical sales drivers, outlet performance metrics, and category-level trends that Blinkit can use to optimize product strategy and outlet-level operations.
